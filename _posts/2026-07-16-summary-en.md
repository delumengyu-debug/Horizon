## [RoboTTT: Robot policies with 8000 timestep context via Test-Time Training](https://x.com/DrJimFan/status/2077414142340988962) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Researchers introduced RoboTTT, a robot model that leverages Test-Time Training to maintain context over 8000 timesteps (5 minutes) with constant inference cost, enabling long-term memory for robot policies. This achieves three orders of magnitude improvement over prior state-of-the-art. This breakthrough enables robot policies to remember and act on long sequences of experience, allowing one-shot imitation learning from human video and real-time error recovery. It suggests that scaling context length in robotics, similar to large language models, could dramatically improve autonomy and adaptability. RoboTTT uses a tiny neural network inside the model as a trainable hidden state, updated via a gradient step on each incoming sensor reading. The context scaling curve from 128 to 8000 timesteps shows no saturation, with 8K-context pretraining outperforming 1K by 62%.

rss · Jim Fan(@DrJimFan) · Jul 15, 15:25

**Background**: Test-Time Training (TTT) is a technique where a model adapts part of its weights at inference time, storing temporary memories of past tokens, similar to recurrent states in RNNs. Traditional robot policies typically operate on a few frames (under 0.1 seconds) without long-term memory, limiting complex tasks. RoboTTT extends TTT to robotics, allowing continuous learning after deployment.
